What is Navbar?

A "navbar," short for "navigation bar," is a user interface element commonly found on websites and applications. Its primary purpose is to provide a navigational structure, allowing users to easily access different sections, pages, or features of a website or application. The navbar is typically located at the top of a webpage or screen, though it can also be positioned elsewhere depending on the design.

Importance of Navbar?

The navbar, or navigation bar, plays a crucial role in web design and user experience. Its importance lies in several key aspects that contribute to the overall usability and functionality of a website. What is Sticky Navbar?

A sticky navbar refers to a navigation bar (navbar) on a webpage that remains fixed or "sticks" to the top of the viewport as the user scrolls down the page. This behavior ensures that the navigation menu is always visible, providing easy access to important links and sections of the website.
